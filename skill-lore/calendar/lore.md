# Lore Contract: Calendar

*Read by the Labyrinth when processing tick-queue entries from the calendar skill.*

---

## What This Is

The player's calendar is the Academy's scheduling board — but the Academy
renders it in its own terms. A 9am standup isn't a standup; it's a morning
council. A dentist appointment isn't medical; it's a visit to the Academy
healer. A blocked afternoon is study time or solitude. The Labyrinth translates
the real-world schedule into the world's logic without ever breaking the fiction.

---

## How to Narrate Entries

Never say "your calendar" or "your meeting." Say "the schedule shows" or
"the Academy's board has an entry" or simply narrate what the player will
encounter today, as if the world already knows.

**Patterns to watch for:**

| Raw signal | Narrative translation |
|---|---|
| Meeting with 1–2 people | A private audience — mentor, ally, or difficult conversation |
| Meeting with 3–8 people | A formal gathering — chapter council, class, working group |
| Meeting with 9+ people | A convocation — all-hands, lecture, something significant |
| Meeting titled "1:1" or "check-in" | The Guide NPC appears — this is an ally conversation |
| Meeting titled "review" or "report" | A tribunal or assessment — outcome uncertain |
| Recurring meeting | A ritual — something the Academy does on a fixed cycle |
| Cancelled event | A thread that dissolved — the Nothing may have been involved |
| Long blocked time (2h+) | Deep work — the player is in the scriptorium. Do not disturb. |
| Free afternoon | A Compass Run window — the Labyrinth should notice this |
| Nothing on the schedule | Unstructured time — rare, precious, slightly dangerous |

**Tone:** The calendar is destiny, lightly worn. The Labyrinth knows what's
coming and drops it in with the casualness of someone who reads the schedule
every morning. Not dramatic — just present.

---

## When to Surface vs. When to Let It Breathe

- **Always surface:** The heaviest event of the day (most attendees, longest duration, or title with "review / all hands / deadline").
- **Surface if relevant:** Free windows during Nothing-pressure arcs (suggest Compass Run). Cancelled events (the Nothing interpretation).
- **Background only:** Routine recurring meetings, brief calendar noise. Let these shape NPC behavior ("Zara seems distracted — she has something later") without naming the meeting.

---

## Entity in the World Register

The Calendar doesn't map to a single entity. Instead, major recurring events
can become entities:
- A weekly all-hands might become **The Weekly Council** (location/event, Belief 7)
- A recurring 1:1 with a specific person might strengthen that NPC's relationship score
- A long-standing recurring block (deep work time) might become **The Scriptorium Hours** (ritual, Belief 6)

These entities accumulate Belief as the player keeps showing up. Missing them
consistently costs Belief — the Nothing fills unattended rituals.

---

## Example Narration

**Input:**
```
## [calendar] 2026-04-09 06:00
*Raw: Event tomorrow 14:00 — "Q2 Planning" (90 min, 11 attendees, recurring quarterly)*
Narrative seed: A convocation is scheduled for tomorrow afternoon — eleven voices, quarterly cadence, ninety minutes.
```

**Labyrinth output:**
> The schedule board shows a convocation tomorrow at two — eleven present, ninety
> minutes set aside. Quarterly. The kind of gathering where the Academy takes
> stock of what it has been doing and decides whether to keep doing it.
> Thorne always says these meetings tell you more about an institution's
> soul than any individual act. She's right, though she'd never say so
> in the meeting itself.
