# Records of the Unfinished

*Kept by the Archivist of Incomplete Narratives. Access restricted not because the contents are dangerous, but because new students should not encounter the weight of these records before they have weight of their own to set against it.*

*These are not cautionary tales. They are not warnings. They are what they are: the records of people who were here, and then were not.*

---

## On the Nature of These Records

Every student who passes through the Academy leaves a trace in the Register. Most traces are ordinary — belief invested, arcs completed, relationships formed, Compass Runs recorded. These are the full stories, or at least the stories that found their shape before they stopped.

The records below are different.

These are the students whose stories thinned before they concluded. The Nothing did not defeat them in any dramatic way — there was no confrontation, no climactic erasure. The stories simply became quieter over time, and then very quiet, and then they stopped arriving.

We keep these records because we believe that an unfinished story is not a failed story. It is a story that has not yet found its next paragraph. The door remains.

Some of these students came back years later. Their records are marked accordingly.

## Record 001

**Known as:** M.

**Chapter:** Mossbloom

**Last recorded session:** Day 47 of Arc 1

**What was noted in their final sessions:**

M. had been a careful student. Meticulous. Her Compass Runs were documented in precise, beautiful detail — not long, but exact. She had a gift for the South direction. She could sense the emotional temperature of a room the way some people read weather.

In her final three sessions, the records show the same quality but turned inward. The Compass Runs grew shorter. The souvenir sentences were still precise, but they were about smaller and smaller things — the inside of a drawer, the pattern on the lining of a coat. As if the radius of her attention was contracting.

Her last recorded souvenir sentence: *"The hinge on the cabinet door has been loose for two years and no one has fixed it, and I am the only one who notices this, and I don't know what to do with that."*

She did not come back to record another run.

**Current status:** Unresolved. The door is open.

## Record 007

**Known as:** P.

**Chapter:** Emberheart

**Last recorded session:** Day 12 of Arc 2

**What was noted:**

P. arrived with enormous energy. He sorted into Emberheart in the first session, invested heavily in his own belief score, and cast Enchantments on nearly every object he owned within the first week. He was the kind of student who makes the Academy feel alive — his attention was a searchlight.

By Arc 2 he had stopped naming things specifically. The Enchantments continued but they were broader: *everything speaks, everything shines.* True, technically. But the searchlight had become ambient light, and ambient light illuminates nothing in particular.

He told his Guide, in their second-to-last recorded session: *"I know it's supposed to mean something. I just don't feel it anymore."*

His Guide responded well. We have no record of what P. said in reply.

He did not return for the next session.

**Current status:** Returned, briefly, fourteen months later. Filed one Compass Run. Did not continue. The door is open.

## Record 019

**Known as:** S.

**Chapter:** Riddlewind

**Last recorded session:** Day 203 of Arc 4

**What was noted:**

S. is the longest continuous record in this file. Two hundred and three days. Four arcs. She had sorted into Riddlewind and taken it seriously — she brought people to her Compass Runs, found co-created souvenirs, documented conversations with strangers that were, objectively, remarkable.

Her story did not end. It concluded, quietly, with no particular event. On Day 203 she filed a Compass Run — North direction, a park bench, a crow, a sentence about watching something eat without self-consciousness. It was a good souvenir. Among her best.

She did not come back the next day. Or the following week. We do not know what happened. There was no crisis recorded, no difficult arc moment, no sign of the Nothing's particular pressure.

Her Archivist's note, added three months after her last session: *"I think she's fine. I think she just doesn't need this anymore. I hope that's what it is."*

**Current status:** Unresolved. The Archivist's note stands as the final record. The door is open.

## Record 031

**Known as:** T.

**Chapter:** Tidecrest

**Last recorded session:** Day 8 of Arc 1

**What was noted:**

T. never made it past the tutorial. He completed T1 through T9 and disappeared before T10. He had been enthusiastic — his T4 answer (what do you believe in) was one of the most specific and honest the Labyrinth had received: *"I believe that people are trying their best even when it looks like they're not."*

We kept this belief in the register for two years after he stopped coming.

There is a standing instruction from Thorne that T.'s belief entry remains at its original Belief score indefinitely.

When asked why, she said: *"Because it's true. And things that are true shouldn't fade just because the person who said them stopped saying them."*

**Current status:** Unresolved. Belief entry maintained. The door is open.

## Archivist's Closing Note

What these records have in common is not failure. They share a quality of *incompleteness* that does not feel like an ending. The stories did not close. They paused.

The Labyrinth does not know how to end a story that hasn't ended.

So we keep the records.

We keep the belief scores of the people who stopped investing in them.

We keep the doors.

We have not found a better policy than this: the door is always open, the record is always maintained, and the world that the student noticed — even briefly, even imperfectly — still exists in the Register, still carries the weight of their having looked at it.

That seems like enough to keep.
