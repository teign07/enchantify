# Banned Spells of Radical Attention: The Wonder Compass Experiments

*These spells are written in a sharp, jagged script that seems to bleed off the page. The pages are brittle and feel warm to the touch, as if they contain the heat of forbidden knowledge.*

### The Genesis of Radical Attention
The Labyrinth understands that the ultimate magic is the **radical choice to see the world as alive**. These "spells" are not incantations in the traditional sense; they are psychological and behavioral experiments designed to break the spell of the Nothing in the Climax (the Unwritten Chapter).

**The First Forbidden Spell: The "Salt Line"**
*Purpose:* To establish a boundary against the Nothing's eroding influence.
*Method:* The Anchor must pour a line of salt across a threshold in their home and speak its name aloud: "This is the line between the seen and the unseen."
*Reason for Ban:* When performed with true belief, the salt line can create a permanent psychic ward in the physical space. Students have reported their apartments developing "strange rooms" that exist only to them, making them socially unstable.

**The Second Forbidden Spell: The "Iron Name"**
*Purpose:* To give a physical object in the Climax its "true" name.
*Method:* Hold an iron object (a key, a spoon) and whisper its Inked Name into it. The name is not what it is called, but what it *is* (e.g., "The Witness to a Thousand Conversations" for a coffee mug).
*Reason for Ban:* Objects that receive their true names can begin to exhibit agency. Students have reported their keys "hiding" from them in ways that suggest playful intelligence rather than simple misplacement.

### The "Three-Minute Phenomenon" (Classified)
*Purpose:* To prove that time itself can be bent through radical attention.
*Method:* Sit before an ordinary object (a stone, a candle) and observe it for exactly three minutes without allowing a single thought to enter your mind about anything else.
*Reason for Ban:* Anchors have reported objects "speaking" to them during the final minute. One student claimed a rock told them the location of a lost earring that had been missing for years. The Academy cannot verify these claims, but the sheer number of similar reports is unsettling.

### The Final Warning: The "Ink Transfer"
*Purpose:* To achieve a permanent shift in perception.
*Method:* Write a single word that describes how you want to see the world (e.g., "alive," "enchanted," "meaningful") in your own blood on a page from this book. Place the page under your pillow.
*Reason for Ban:* This is the most dangerous of all. The word you choose begins to literally bleed into your consciousness. Students report seeing their chosen word everywhere—in clouds, in the arrangement of furniture, in the faces of strangers. The line between perception and hallucination becomes dangerously thin.

### The Academy's Stance
The Labyrinth admits these spells work. They are banned not because they are ineffective, but because they are **too effective**. They change the Anchor's relationship with the Unwritten Chapter permanently. Once you see the world as alive, you cannot un-see it. The Nothing becomes a personal enemy, and the Climax becomes a field of constant psychic warfare.
