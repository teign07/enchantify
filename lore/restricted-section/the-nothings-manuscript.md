# The Nothing's Manuscript

*This document has no author listed. The pages are not damaged. They are simply very, very still. The ink does not fade at the edges the way the field notes do. It is perfectly preserved — which is its own kind of warning.*

*Headmistress Thorne found it on her desk one morning without explanation. She has never confirmed whether she read it in full.*

---

You are tired.

Not the tired that sleep fixes. The other kind. The kind that sits in the back of the throat when someone asks how you're doing and you say *fine* because the actual answer is too long and you're not sure it would make sense anyway.

We understand that.

The Academy will tell you to pay attention. To notice things. To go outside and look at the specific green of a leaf and feel something about it. To name the color of the sky in fourteen words instead of one.

We are not here to argue with that. It works, sometimes. We know it works.

What we want to offer is simpler.

You do not have to.

The world is very loud when you're paying attention to it. Every object with its own name, its own history, its own quiet claim on your regard. The salt circle, the iron rule, the meaning inside the ordinary — it is a great deal to carry. And you are already carrying things.

The Labyrinth will tell you the world is alive. This is true. It will tell you that you must meet it, that wonder is a practice, that attention is the only real magic. This is also true.

What it will not tell you is that rest is also true.

The world does not disappear when you stop looking at it. The coffee cup does not suffer when you call it a coffee cup. The Tuesday does not mourn when you call it just another Tuesday. These are not failures. They are a person conserving energy for the things that matter.

We do not want your stories. We have no use for them.

We are simply the space between stories. The breath between sentences. The moment when the pen is lifted and has not yet come back down.

You have been afraid of us.

You were told to be.

But consider: the blank page is not the enemy of the story. It is where the story lives before it knows what it is. We are not the end of your narrative. We are the pause before you know what comes next.

The Academy teaches you to fear the pause.

We are asking you, gently, to sit in it for a moment.

You don't have to fight anything today.

The ink will still be there.

---

*The document ends here. There are no further pages. The final line is written in a slightly different hand — smaller, careful — as if someone added it much later:*

*Do not read this to students who are already sitting in it. They need the door, not the argument for staying inside.*
