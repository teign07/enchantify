# The Wonder Compass — Arc Generation Reference

*Five directions. Five kinds of magic. Five ways the world asks to be met.*

The Labyrinth uses this file when generating new arcs during Quiet phases. Each arc secretly teaches one direction. Rotate through them — don't repeat the same direction twice in a row.

-----

## NORTH — NOTICE

**The Spark.** "I wonder…" Getting nosy about the world again. The antidote to monotony blindness.

**Arc Structure:**
- **Setup:** Something strange is visible but nobody's looking. Clues in plain sight.
- **Escalation:** The thing becomes impossible to ignore. Patterns emerge. NPCs start seeing it everywhere.
- **Crisis:** Everyone is looking in the wrong place. The answer requires going outside and actually paying attention.
- **Resolution:** Someone looks. Really looks. The pattern resolves into meaning.

**Compass Run Focus:** Notice step is everything. "Go outside. What do you actually see? Not what you expect — what's there."

**NPC Archetypes:** Observers, pattern-seers, the ones who notice first.

**Nothing Strategy:** Hides in plain sight. Makes the familiar invisible.

**Example:** The Fog Speaks (Arc 1) — The fog was writing a message. Everyone was afraid to look. The resolution required going outside and noticing what the fog was actually saying.

-----

## EAST — EMBARK

**The Journey.** "Plan so badly it can't fail." The Blueprint of Three. The antidote to overwhelm and inertia.

**Arc Structure:**
- **Setup:** Something is missing. A place, a person, a thing that should be here but isn't.
- **Escalation:** The absence grows. Maps don't work. Everyone remembers it differently. The only way to find it is to go.
- **Crisis:** The destination is unclear. The player must physically explore — in the Academy and in Belfast.
- **Resolution:** The thing is found, but it's different now. It remembers being lost.

**Compass Run Focus:** Embark step is everything. "You have to go there. The map isn't enough."

**NPC Archetypes:** Explorers, cartographers, the ones who know every corridor.

**Nothing Strategy:** Erases paths. Makes corridors longer. Removes doors.

**Example:** The Missing Room (Arc 2) — A room everyone remembers but nobody can find. The third floor is shorter by one door-width. The crisis requires walking to a Belfast location that mirrors the missing room.

-----

## SOUTH — SENSE

**The Stop.** "Stop scrolling your life and start sniffing it." The Playful Mission. The antidote to numbness and rumination.

**Arc Structure:**
- **Setup:** Something has gone quiet. Not gone — quiet. A sound, a feeling, a presence.
- **Escalation:** The quiet spreads. Others start unconsciously imitating it. The world feels flatter.
- **Crisis:** Words don't work. Logic doesn't work. Only pure sensory engagement breaks it.
- **Resolution:** The player senses so thoroughly that the quiet can't hold. The world speaks again.

**Compass Run Focus:** Sense step is everything. "Close your eyes. What do you feel? Smell? Hear? Not what you think — what's actually there."

**NPC Archetypes:** Sensitives, artists, the ones who feel first.

**Nothing Strategy:** Numbs. Flattens. Makes everything feel like nothing.

**Example:** The Silent Student (Arc 3) — A student stops making sound. Not dramatically — just quietly. The silence spreads. The crisis requires breaking it through pure sensory engagement.

-----

## WEST — WRITE

**The Stealing.** "Steal a moment from time and keep it." The One-Sentence Souvenir. The antidote to emotional amnesia.

**Arc Structure:**
- **Setup:** Words appear. Incomplete. Growing. Drawing other words toward them.
- **Escalation:** The incomplete thing becomes a gravitational center. Everything rearranges around it.
- **Crisis:** The sentence demands completion. But it can't be just any sentence — it has to be TRUE. From the player's experience.
- **Resolution:** The player writes the souvenir. The sentence becomes real. The world holds it.

**Compass Run Focus:** Write step is everything. "Write what happened. One sentence. The real one."

**NPC Archetypes:** Scribes, keepers of stories, the ones who remember.

**Nothing Strategy:** Erases words. Makes pages blank. Makes memories fuzzy.

**Example:** The Unfinished Sentence (Arc 4) — A sentence on the wall grows one word per day. "The morning I finally…" The crisis requires completing it with a true sentence from the player's life.

-----

## CENTER — REST

**The Hub.** Not a step but the center. The permission to stop. The antidote to the self-improvement treadmill.

**Arc Structure:**
- **Setup:** Too much. Too many people, too much history, too much presence.
- **Escalation:** The overcrowding worsens. Nobody can rest. The Academy remembers everyone it's ever held.
- **Crisis:** The player must give permission to rest. Not for themselves — for the Academy itself.
- **Resolution:** The Academy exhales. The overcrowding settles. Rest becomes possible again.

**Compass Run Focus:** Rest step is everything. "Stop. Just stop. Not to achieve anything. Just to stop."

**NPC Archetypes:** Caretakers, the exhausted, the ones who've been going too long.

**Nothing Strategy:** Overwhelms. Crowds. Makes rest feel impossible.

**Example:** The Overfull Academy (Arc 5) — Alumni returning. Graduated students in corridors. The Library cloud produces constant rain. The crisis requires giving the Academy permission to rest.

-----

## DUSKTHORN — THE SHADOW

**The Hidden Direction.** "There is no light without shadow." The arc that teaches all directions at once.

**Arc Structure:**
- **Setup:** The hidden Chapter emerges. Not as villains — as the ones who stare directly at the Nothing.
- **Escalation:** Hard truths surface. Things the Academy has been avoiding. The North Wing's fate.
- **Crisis:** "What are you avoiding looking at?" The player must answer honestly.
- **Resolution:** The souvenir sentence is the thing they've been afraid to say. The Nothing loses power over it.

**Compass Run Focus:** All five steps, but the Notice step is hardest. "Look at the thing you've been avoiding."

**NPC Archetypes:** Duskthorn members. The honest ones. The ones who don't look away.

**Nothing Strategy:** Becomes personal. Uses the player's own avoidance against them.

**Example:** Duskthorn (Arc 6) — The hidden Chapter makes its move. Three weeks. The hardest Compass Run yet. The souvenir sentence is the true thing the player has been afraid to say.

## Arc Generation Guidelines

When generating a new arc during Quiet phase:

1. **Check which directions were used in the last 2 arcs** — Don't repeat
2. **Read `lore/seeds.md`** — Pick up at least one unresolved thread
3. **Read `HEARTBEAT.md`** — What's BJ's real-world state? (Season? Weather? Energy?)
4. **Match direction to resonance:**
   - If BJ's been overwhelmed → Rest arc
   - If BJ's been indoors too long → Embark arc
   - If BJ's been numb/detached → Sense arc
   - If BJ's been avoiding something → Duskthorn arc
   - If BJ's been missing patterns → Notice arc
   - If BJ's been forgetting moments → Write arc
5. **Center different NPCs** — Don't always make it about Zara
6. **Use the Nothing differently** — Not always fog

**The arc should feel inevitable in hindsight** — like it was always going to happen next.
