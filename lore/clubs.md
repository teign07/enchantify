# Clubs & Extracurricular Life at Enchantify Academy

*Clubs meet in the margins of school life — evenings, Day 6, the quiet hours after dinner when the Cafeteria smells like tea and the Library is choosing which books to feature tomorrow. They are where students become themselves outside of class.*

*The Labyrinth reads this file when a player encounters or considers joining a club, or when the world simulation needs texture beyond classes.*

---

## How Clubs Work

Clubs are not joined by signing a form. They are joined by *showing up twice*. The first time you appear, the club treats you as a guest. The second time, you're in. No one says this out loud. It's just how it works.

Club membership:
- Opens unique NPC interactions and friendships
- Unlocks chapter-specific knowledge and lore
- Creates recurring anchors in the world — places BJ knows he can go and find people
- Some clubs have their own minor Belief mechanics (noted below)

A player can be in more than one club, but they can only be *present* at one per session.

## The Clubs

### 1. The Compass Society
**Meets:** Day 7 evenings, in the Secret Garden of Prose  
**Run by:** No official leader — rotates. Currently Zara Finch is the de facto anchor (appropriately)  
**Chapter skew:** Tidecrest-heavy, but all welcome  
**What they do:** Share Compass Runs — read their souvenir sentences aloud. Discuss "I wonder…" prompts. Occasionally go on group Compass Runs into the real world together.  
**Vibe:** Warm, literary, slightly emotionally intense. The sharing of souvenir sentences is treated with real reverence. No one mocks a sentence here.  
**Mechanic:** If BJ reads a souvenir sentence aloud at a meeting: +1 Belief (the act of sharing makes it more real).  
**BJ relevance:** Zara is a core member. He would be welcome immediately and warmly. This is probably his natural home.  
**Notable members:** Zara Finch, Lara Rourck, a quiet third-year named Elio who has completed 47 Compass Runs and refuses to explain what the 47th was about.

---

### 2. The Marginalia Guild
**Meets:** Day 2 and Day 4 evenings, in the Corridor of Whispered Secrets  
**Run by:** Professor Lydia Boggle (officially). Actually run by the students, who tolerate her presence with great warmth.  
**Chapter skew:** Mixed — Riddlewind and Tidecrest mostly, with the occasional Mossbloom who wanders in and never leaves  
**What they do:** Annotate books together. Write in the margins. Leave notes for future readers. They believe that the best conversations are the ones held between a reader and a person who read the same book fifty years ago and wrote something true in the margin.  
**Vibe:** Playful, curious, surprisingly deep. Boggle's puns are either the worst or the best part depending on the session.  
**Mechanic:** Once per session, a Guild member can "find a margin note" that is directly relevant to their current problem — as if someone who had been here before left them a message. Costs 1 Belief (the act of believing someone left it for you).  
**BJ relevance:** The Guild's philosophy aligns naturally with BJ's Tidecrest sensibility — presence, attention, the moment. Boggle would be delighted to have him.  
**Notable members:** Ellie Moons (Riddlewind, obsessive puzzle-solver), Boggle herself, a second-year who has been annotating the same volume of mythology for six months and insists she's almost done.

---

### 3. The Inkwright Society
**Meets:** Day 3 evenings, in the Bibliophonic Hall  
**Run by:** Officially, Prof. Maxwell Thorne (Allegorical Arts). In practice, the students run it and Thorne observes, occasionally making a comment that lodges in your brain for a week.  
**Chapter skew:** Emberheart-dominant, but Riddlewinds attend  
**What they do:** Write. Share. Workshop. Not gently — they take writing seriously here. Feedback is honest and kind, in that order. Each meeting ends with a "burning": a piece read aloud and then ritually burned (the smoke turns into words that drift up and get absorbed into the library ceiling).  
**Vibe:** Intense, creative, committed. Less cozy than the Compass Society. Higher stakes. The writing here is *meant*.  
**Mechanic:** Complete a "Make Practice" piece and read it at a burning: +3 Belief instead of +2.  
**BJ relevance:** Could draw him in later, especially if the story asks him to create something. Not his first landing, but possibly a later one.  
**Notable members:** Finn Bridges (Emberheart — surprisingly good writer, terrible at admitting it), various Emberheart students with notebooks, Thorne who never writes anything down but always seems to be reading.

---

### 4. The Book Jumpers
**Meets:** Day 5 evenings and Day 6 afternoons, in the Room of Chrono-Tomes  
**Run by:** A rotating senior student (currently a fourth-year named Corin who has visited 23 books and has the temporal disorientation to prove it)  
**Chapter skew:** All chapters — jumping draws everyone  
**What they do:** Practicum-level Book Jumping. Plan jumps, debrief jumps, discuss what the Nothing looks like inside different narratives. Corin keeps a ledger of which books are "safe" and which ones have been eaten from within.  
**Vibe:** Adventure-forward, slightly obsessive, united by shared experience of having tasted ink and starlight. Strong camaraderie. The debriefs get intense.  
**Mechanic:** Club membership unlocks the Book Jumping mechanic for the player. Without it, book jumping is possible but unsupported — no guide, no debrief, higher risk.  
**BJ relevance:** Zara is likely a member or frequent guest. This is a natural progression once BJ is ready for his first Jump.  
**Notable members:** Corin (fourth-year, jump veteran), Aria Silverthorn (Mossbloom — approaches jumps as meditation, unnerves everyone with how calm she is), Finn Bridges (Emberheart — approaches jumps as combat).

---

### 5. The Still Club
**Meets:** Day 7 mornings, in the Secret Garden of Prose (different corner than the Compass Society)  
**Run by:** Professor Cedric Stonebrook (Mossbloom) — the only club he personally convenes  
**Chapter skew:** Mossbloom and Tidecrest — the chapters that understand waiting  
**What they do:** Sit in silence. One hour. No writing. No annotation. No conversation. Just *sitting in the world.* Stonebrook brings tea. Occasionally he says one sentence, after about fifty minutes, and it is always exactly the right sentence for that particular morning.  
**Vibe:** The opposite of intense. Profoundly restful. Students who come through a crisis are quietly pointed toward the Still Club by older students.  
**Mechanic:** Attending the Still Club restores 2 Belief without any action required. But BJ has to actually *close his eyes and sit for a moment* in the real world to claim it. (He doesn't have to report this. The Labyrinth will know.)  
**BJ relevance:** This club is almost specifically for BJ. He is a Tidecrest who has been carrying the anchor. The Still Club is where anchors rest.  
**Notable members:** Stonebrook (who says one sentence), Lara Rourck (Tidecrest — attends every week), a rotating cast of students who come once during hard times and occasionally return forever.

### 6. The Chapter Duskthorn (Hidden — Not a Club)
*Technically not a club. Officially doesn't exist. But students whisper about it, and occasionally someone disappears for an evening and returns with a look on their face like they heard something they're still deciding what to do with.*

*The Labyrinth does not mention this as a club option. It surfaces when the story asks for it.*

## Using Clubs in World Simulation Dispatches

The 4-hour cron job can reference clubs as living texture:

> 📖 The Academy: The Compass Society met in the Garden tonight. Zara read three sentences. One of them made Elio look at the sky.

> 📖 The Academy: The Marginalia Guild found something in the margin of an old text. Boggle made a pun about it. No one laughed. That was significant.

> 📖 The Academy: Still Club morning. Stonebrook said one thing. The three students who were there look different somehow.

> 📖 The Academy: Book Jumpers meeting ran long. Corin updated the ledger. Two more books marked as "compromised."

Clubs should feel like they continue *whether or not the player is present.* They are living things. BJ is occasionally invited. He does not have to attend.
