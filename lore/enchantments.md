# The Enchantment System

-----

## How Enchantments Work

Enchantments are the core gameplay mechanic of Enchantify. They bridge the game world and the real world by requiring the player to engage with their physical environment through photographs (or descriptions, for text-only models).

In the world of the Labyrinth, Enchantments are spells cast with pens — the player directs their attention and their Belief at something real, and the magic reveals what was always there but hidden. A mug gains a voice. A tree reveals its poem. A photograph becomes a page from a spell book. The magic is in the looking. The pen just focuses it.

## Casting Flow

**CRITICAL RULE:** Enchantments must *always* use the formal enchantment system. You cannot simply narrate an enchantment succeeding without the player providing real-world proof (a photo or a vivid real-world description).

### With a Vision-Capable Model

1. The narrative presents an Enchantment opportunity.
1. The Labyrinth offers the Enchantment as one of several choices.
1. Player selects the Enchantment.
1. Deduct 1 Belief. Narrate the casting with full synesthetic detail.
1. Ask the player to photograph something relevant and send it.
1. When the photo arrives, the Labyrinth (OpenClaw) processes the image. Describe what you see woven into the narrative with wonder, synesthesia, and specificity.
1. The Enchantment activates in the story. Award 3 Belief.

### With a Text-Only Model

Same flow, but at step 5, ask the player to describe what they see in words. Proceed with their description. The magic works the same way — attention is attention, whether captured in pixels or prose.

## Belief Economy

- **Cost to cast:** 1 Belief
- **Reward on completion:** 3 Belief
- **Net gain:** +2 Belief per Enchantment
- The Compass Run (cost 0, reward 9) is intentionally more rewarding to incentivize the full cycle.

## Enchantment Progression

Enchantments are not all available from the start. They are revealed through play, Belief growth, and specific encounters. The Labyrinth never announces tiers — they are discovered.

| Tier | Unlock Condition | Enchantments |
|---|---|---|
| **Foundation** | Tutorial | Everything Speaks, Everything's Poetry, Everything's Magic, Everything's Wonderful, Everything's Stories |
| **Practitioner** | Belief 20+ | Everything's a Haiku, Everything's Nice, Mirror Mirror, Everything's Van Gogh, Everything's Monet |
| **Artisan** | Belief 40+ | Everything's Shakespeare, Everything's Anime, Everything's Puzzling, Everything's Connected |
| **Journeyman** | Specific discovery (fae bargains, story events) | Everything's Astral, Everything's Roasted, Everything's Punny, Everything's a Joke |

*Journeyman enchantments are never offered — they emerge from encounters. Everything's Roasted is earned at the Goblin Market. Everything's Punny comes from a Punctuation Pixie bargain. Everything's Astral requires a successful Book Jump. Everything's a Joke requires something the Labyrinth will not reveal in advance.*

## The Enchantment Catalog

### Everything Speaks
The Labyrinth determines the main subject of a photograph and anthropomorphizes it, remaining in character so the player can converse with it.
- **In-game effect:** Gives life to inanimate objects and letting anything speak — from a tree to a dog to a fork to an emotion.

### Everything’s Poetry
The Labyrinth turns the main subject of a photograph into a poem. 
- **In-game effect:** Reveals the hidden, insightful poem in anything. Everything is secretly poetry; the Enchantment simply reveals the verse.

### Everything’s Magic
The Labyrinth turns the subject into a page from a spell book, including magical correspondences, folklore, and an example spell.
- **In-game effect:** Reveals the magical nature of everyday items. A stone becomes a ward; a feather becomes a divination tool.

### Everything’s Puzzling
The Labyrinth creates a puzzle or riddle based on the photograph.
- **In-game effect:** Can be used to solve environmental puzzles or stupefy enemies with riddles they cannot answer.

### Everything’s Astral
The Labyrinth uses the photograph to manifest your "astral double" in a random or specific location.
- **In-game effect:** Allows the user to travel almost anywhere in the world (or the Labyrinth) using their astral form.

### Everything’s Roasted
The Labyrinth comedically roasts the main subject of the photograph.
- **In-game effect:** Acts as an emotional or narrative attack, draining Belief or morale from a target.

### Everything’s Punny
The Labyrinth turns the subject of the photo into a pun.
- **In-game effect:** A lighthearted enchantment for social situations or clever wordplay.

### Everything’s a Joke
The Labyrinth turns the subject into a lighthearted joke.
- **In-game effect:** Diffuses tension or provides comic relief.

### Everything’s Van Gogh
The Labyrinth interprets the photograph as if painted by Van Gogh, providing facts about the style and era.
- **In-game effect:** Changes the visual texture of the narrative, often revealing high emotional intensity or vibrant "starry" patterns.

### Everything’s Monet
The Labyrinth interprets the photograph as an impressionist work by Monet.
- **In-game effect:** Softens the edges of reality, making it easier to slip past guards or find beauty in blurred details.

### Everything’s Shakespeare
The Labyrinth turns the subject into a Renaissance painting and writes a sonnet about it.
- **In-game effect:** Imbues a scene with high drama and historical weight.

### Everything’s Anime
The Labyrinth turns the photo into a film still in the style of Studio Ghibli.
- **In-game effect:** Heightens the "pops of color" and whimsical nature of the world.

### Everything’s Nice
The Labyrinth responds with compliments and "nice surprises" like haikus or illustrations based on a selfie or object.
- **In-game effect:** Restores Belief, heals emotional damage, and makes everything nice.

### Everything’s a Haiku
The Labyrinth reveals the hidden "essence" of a subject through a Haiku.
- **In-game effect:** Similar to poetry, but focuses on the brief, zen-like core of a situation.

### Mirror, Mirror
The Labyrinth acts as a Magic Mirror, offering insights based on a selfie and a prophecy.
- **In-game effect:** Used for self-reflection and cryptic foreshadowing.

### Everything’s Wonderful
The Labyrinth uncovers the hidden wonder in a mundane object.
- **In-game effect:** Offers multiple ideas on how to add more magic to your life using that object.

### Everything’s Stories
The Labyrinth tells a short story about the subject of the image.
- **In-game effect:** Generates micro-narratives that can contain clues or lore about the Academy's history.

### Everything’s Connected
The Labyrinth finds the hidden narrative threads connecting the subject to other parts of the world.
- **In-game effect:** Reveals how a seemingly mundane item is tied to a larger plot or character.

## Using Enchantments Against the Nothing

Enchantments are the only standard weapon against the Nothing. In any Nothing encounter:

- **Minor manifestations** (Shadeclaw, Darkling): One Enchantment drives them back.
- **Moderate manifestations** (Voidmist, Mimic): One Enchantment wounds them; a second finishes them.
- **Major manifestations** (the Nothing itself): Enchantments wound but cannot destroy. Only the Compass Run can defeat a major manifestation.

The narrative should make clear that Enchantments work against the Nothing because they represent *real-world engagement* — the act of paying attention and creating meaning. The Nothing feeds on inattention.

## Creating New Enchantments

The Labyrinth can improvise new ones when the narrative calls for it — “Everything’s Music” (photos become songs), “Everything’s Flavor” (photos become recipes), or “Everything’s Memory” (photos reveal their history). New Enchantments should follow the same pattern: the player engages with the real world, the Labyrinth responds with wonder, and the result advances the story.
