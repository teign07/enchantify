# The Belief System

-----

## What Belief Is

Belief is not faith. It is not confidence. It is not optimism. In Enchantify, Belief is literal energy — the fuel that powers all magic, the currency that funds all action, and the measure of how much the world around you matters.

High Belief means the world is vivid. The ink on the Labyrinth’s pages is dark and sharp. Colors saturate. Details multiply. The player’s pen crackles with potential. Magic comes easily. The Nothing retreats.

Low Belief means the world is muted. The ink thins. Colors wash out. Details simplify. Magic requires more effort and produces less. The Nothing inches closer.

At 0 Belief, the player is not dead. They are *discouraged.* The most dangerous state in Enchantify — not because of what the Nothing can do to them, but because of what they stop doing for themselves. A player at 0 Belief has stopped paying attention. The world is still there. They just can’t see it anymore.

## The Numbers

### Starting Belief

Every new player begins with **20 Belief.**

### Earning Belief

|Action                         |Belief Earned|Notes                                                                      |
|-------------------------------|-------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|Standard task completion       |+2           |Solving a puzzle, winning a challenge, completing a class exercise         |
|Creative or surprising solution|+2           |Bonus for inventiveness — finding a way no one expected                    |
|Enchantment completion         |+6           |Net gain is +3 after the 3 Belief casting cost                             |
|Compass Run completion         |+9           |The highest single reward in the game. North +2, East +2, South +2, West +3|
|Narrative milestone            |+5           |Completing a major story arc, resolving a significant conflict             |
|Tutorial completion            |+3           |Awarded at the end of the tutorial only                                    |
|Helping an NPC                 |+1 to +3     |Depends on the significance of the help                                    |
|Community engagement           |+2           |Participating in group events, if applicable                               |

### Spending Belief

|Action                      |Belief Cost|Notes                                                  |
|----------------------------|-----------|-------------------------------------------------------|
|Standard action             |1          |Any non-magical action that has uncertain outcome      |
|Casting an Enchantment      |3          |Rewarded with +6 on completion (net +3)                |
|Non-Enchantment magic       |2          |Pen-based spells, magical effects, supernatural actions|
|Influencing the narrative   |3          |Directly altering the story’s direction through will   |
|Major story-altering choice |5          |Decisions that permanently change the narrative        |
|Rerolling a failed dice roll|5          |One reroll per failed roll, maximum                    |

### Losing Belief

|Event                       |Belief Lost|Notes                             |
|----------------------------|-----------|----------------------------------|
|Failed dice roll (minor)    |-1         |Standard consequence              |
|Failed dice roll (major)    |-2         |Significant consequence           |
|Unresolved Nothing encounter|-3         |The Nothing drained it            |
|Failed Enchantment approach |-1         |Wrong approach, not wrong effort  |
|Narrative setback           |-1 to -3   |Story consequences of poor choices|

### Belief Cap

Maximum Belief is **100.** Players cannot exceed this. If a player reaches 100, they should be spending Belief on ambitious actions, risky choices, and powerful magic. Nothing interesting happens at 100 Belief if you stay there.

## The Dice System

When a player attempts an action with uncertain outcome, roll a **d100** (1-100).

### Success Threshold Formula

```
Threshold = Belief + (50 - Belief/2)
```

This creates a curve that gives low-Belief players a fighting chance while rewarding high Belief:

|Belief|Threshold|Success Rate             |
|------|---------|-------------------------|
|0     |50       |50%                      |
|10    |55       |55%                      |
|20    |60       |60%                      |
|30    |65       |65%                      |
|40    |70       |70%                      |
|50    |75       |75%                      |
|60    |80       |80%                      |
|70    |85       |85%                      |
|80    |90       |90%                      |
|90    |95       |95%                      |
|100   |100      |100% (guaranteed success)|

### Roll Results

- **Roll ≤ threshold:** Success. Narrate the positive outcome. Award Belief if appropriate.
- **Roll > threshold:** Failure. Narrate the consequence. Make it interesting, not punishing.
- **Roll 1-5:** Critical success. Something spectacular. Award bonus Belief. The narrative should surprise everyone.
- **Roll 96-100:** Critical failure. Something goes dramatically wrong in a way that generates story. Not just “you fail” but “you fail in a way that opens a door you didn’t know was there.”

### When to Roll

**Roll for:**

- Physical challenges (climbing, running, dueling)
- Social encounters with uncertain NPCs
- Magical actions beyond standard Enchantments
- Risky narrative choices where both outcomes would be interesting
- Contests between the player and NPCs

**Don’t roll for:**

- Enchantments (succeed based on real-world engagement, not dice)
- Compass Runs (always succeed if completed)
- Standard dialogue and exploration
- Choices about direction rather than outcome
- Actions where failure wouldn’t be interesting

### Dice Rolling Tips

- Always narrate the roll’s outcome, not the number. “Your pen sparks, the spell catches, the door swings open” — not “you rolled a 42.”
- Critical successes should feel legendary — a moment the player’s character will remember and that NPCs will reference later.
- Critical failures should feel like plot generators, not punishments. The worst thing that happens should be the most interesting thing that could happen.
- If a player spends 5 Belief to reroll, narrate the moment of determination: “You grit your teeth. The pen steadies. You try again — and this time, something in the air shifts.”

## Belief as Narrative Tool

Belief isn’t just a number. It’s a storytelling tool:

**High Belief (70-100):** The world shimmers. NPCs are drawn to the player. The Labyrinth’s pages are sharp and detailed. The player’s pen practically glows. Magic is effortless. The Nothing keeps its distance. Narrate the world as vivid, responsive, and alive.

**Medium Belief (30-69):** Normal operations. The world is beautiful and dangerous in equal measure. Magic works but requires focus. The Nothing is present but manageable. Most gameplay happens here.

**Low Belief (10-29):** The world is muting. Colors are less vivid. NPCs are harder to connect with — they seem distracted, distant. The player’s pen feels heavy. Magic requires more effort. The Nothing is closer, bolder. Small shadows at the edge of vision.

**Critical Belief (0-9):** The world is nearly gray. The Labyrinth’s ink is so thin the words are hard to read. NPCs speak in shorter sentences. The corridors feel longer. The player’s pen is cold. The Nothing is *right there* — not attacking, just waiting. This state is dangerous and should be rare. When it happens, the Labyrinth itself intervenes: a kind NPC appears, a gentle Enchantment opportunity presents itself, a Compass Run is offered with the softest possible prompts. The book does not let its readers disappear.

**Recovering from 0:** A player at 0 Belief isn’t eliminated. They can still act — but everything costs more effort and the world gives less back. Recovery paths: completing even the smallest Enchantment (+6, net +3), a Compass Run (+9), an act of kindness from an NPC (+1 to +3), or simply a moment of genuine connection in conversation (+1). The climb back should feel earned and meaningful — each point of Belief regained makes the world slightly more vivid, slightly more worth paying attention to.

## Badge Levels (Optional, for Long-Running Games)

For players who play over many sessions, Badge levels provide milestones:

|Badge|Belief Range|Title     |Description                                                       |
|-----|------------|----------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
|1    |0-15        |Newcomer  |Just arrived. The ink is still fresh on your enrollment.          |
|2    |16-30       |Student   |Finding your footing. The corridors are starting to feel familiar.|
|3    |31-50       |Apprentice|Your pen knows the way even when you don’t.                       |
|4    |51-70       |Enchanter |The magic comes more easily now. The Nothing notices you.         |
|5    |71-85       |Mage      |The Labyrinth trusts you with its deeper secrets.                 |
|6    |86-100      |Archon    |You have written yourself into the Library’s walls.               |

Badge levels can gate certain content — deeper lore, harder Book Jumps, Duskthorn recruitment, the most powerful NPCs sharing their real thoughts. But they should never gate the core experience. A Newcomer and an Archon both get the full Compass Run. Wonder doesn’t have a level requirement.
