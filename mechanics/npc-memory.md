# NPC Memory — Proactive Surfacing Rules

NPCs are not NPCs. They have inner lives. When an NPC has done something
since they last appeared in play — researched a topic, assigned a quest,
invested Belief, or had their Belief eroded — they carry that with them.
The Director's Slate CAST layer flags these with `[HAS: type · detail]`.

---

## When CAST Shows a Flag

The flag means: this NPC has done something recently that they might
naturally bring up. Not "they will announce it" — they might weave it in,
reference it obliquely, or let it show in how they're behaving.

**Surface within the next 1–2 appearances of that NPC.** After that, the
moment has passed. Don't force it if the conversation doesn't open for it.

---

## By Action Type

### `research` — NPC delivered a research note

The NPC went to the Margin-Glass and found something. They know it's been
delivered. They might:
- Ask if it arrived, casually, not urgently
- Reference something they found while doing it: *"I went looking for what
  you asked about — found more than I expected."*
- If the player has an open quest connected to the topic, the NPC may press
  gently: *"Have you been to look yet?"*

The NPC does not repeat the research content. They acknowledge it happened.

### `elective` — NPC assigned a quest

The NPC wrote an elective and it's sitting in the Inside Cover. They know
the player has it. They might:
- Reference the location or object the quest involves, in passing
- Show curiosity about whether the player has gone yet
- Add weight to the ask: *"I've been thinking about what I asked you to find.
  There's something I didn't say in the note."*
- If the quest is overdue (been in the Cover a long time), the NPC feels it —
  not angry, but slightly expectant

### `belief_invest` — NPC invested Belief in their talisman

The NPC gave something of themselves to their chapter's cause. They're more
invested now, literally. They might:
- Feel more energized or purposeful than usual
- Reference their chapter with slightly more weight than normal
- Show up in a location connected to their chapter more naturally
- If the talisman is contested, they might be watching it more closely

Don't announce the investment. Show it through presence and attention.

### `belief_fell` — Belief dropped significantly

Something eroded this NPC or entity. Could be Nothing pressure, could be
circumstances, could be depletion. They might:
- Seem slightly less defined than usual — softer edges, flatter affect
- Be slightly absent even when present: *"Zara is at her window but not
  reading — just watching, like the courtyard forgot to do anything."*
- Reference fatigue or a sense that something is slipping
- If it's a Location entity: the place feels slightly hollow, like a room
  that was occupied and isn't anymore

This is the Nothing's work made visible. Don't explain it. Let the player
notice.

---

## Rules

1. **One surfacing per action.** Once an NPC has brought something up, the
   entry's relevance expires. Don't repeat.
2. **Organic entry points only.** Wait for a natural beat — don't insert it
   if the scene has no opening. Better to skip it than force it.
3. **Never announce the mechanism.** The NPC doesn't say "I researched X."
   They say something that shows they did.
4. **Multiple flags on one NPC:** take the most recent and most narratively
   interesting. One thing per appearance, not a list.
5. **belief_fell on a named location** (not a person): let the environment
   carry it — architecture, light quality, NPC behavior in that space.
